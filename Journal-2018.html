Comics April 28th, 2018

My literary career began with comic books. I learned to read from Superman, Batman and the Amazing Spiderman, along with Aquaman, Wonderwoman, The Flash and, especially, The Joker. My artistic career started long before, of course. Like any child, I loved to draw and color. I remember once my mother bringing me a Casper the Friendly Ghost Coloring Book from the grocery store when I was ill. My first paint set came from The Psychedelic Shop on Haight Ashbury in San Francisco.

I'm afraid I can't say I've followed comics much as an adult. I don't plan my vacations around COMICON; in fact, getting the link was the first time I've ever been to the site. I once started exploring a shop in Virginia Beach (or was it Chesapeake?), but it didn't take. Purely coincidentally, I just passed another comic books shop, The Tangled Web, on the way to a friend's housewarming party recently. Odd, their web site has to be one of those web site production sites, which the owner has been remiss in completing.

What do you expect? Comics have always been associated with slackers. What surprises me more is that it is obviously on of those web building sites. I would think these guys would be wizzes at web development. There home page isn't bad.

My renewal of interest in comics did not arise from the discovery of a comic book store, however. There is an Alex Ross exhibition in town.

Ross was a disciple of Andrew Loomis, who taught at the American Academy of Art, though much earlier than the years Ross attended (Loomis died in 1959).
